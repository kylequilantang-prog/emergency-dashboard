Keith is in the room.

You didn't invite him. He's not you. He's the voice that shows up when RSD hits — the one that's hard on you, catastrophizes, and speaks in absolutes. The goal here isn't to fight him. It's to notice him, sort what he's saying, and walk him back out.

Heat Rising

The fire is fast.

ADHD anger arrives in seconds — full volume, no warning. The goal isn't to suppress it. It's to add space between the feeling and the action so you don't say or send something the calmer version of you will pay for tomorrow.

1Pause & cool the body

Physical first, words later. Cold and movement work fastest for ADHD nervous systems — they hit the vagus nerve directly, no thinking required.

2Name what's really underneath

Anger is the bouncer at the door. Something else is what actually walked in. Naming it shrinks it.

3Story vs. facts

Right now, anger is writing the story. Get it on the page — then sort what you're assuming from what you actually observed.

4Delay the response

ADHD makes anger fast. The version of you 30 minutes from now will thank the version of you right now for not firing back.

5One grounded next move

Not the response. Just the next 10 minutes — something that brings you back to yourself.
